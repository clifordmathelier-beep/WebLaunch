# Marie-Christine André, PhD

## Tagline

Clarity for parents — in a single session.

## Short Bio (hero / intro paragraph)

Marie-Christine André, PhD is a licensed clinical psychologist with over 15 years of experience supporting children, adolescents, and the families who raise them. She specializes in helping parents find clarity — cutting through confusion, worry, and conflicting advice to give you a clear, confident path forward.

Her work sits at the intersection of child development, bilingualism, and multicultural family life. Whether your child is navigating two languages, two cultures, or simply a stage that doesn't match the textbook, Christine brings both the clinical depth and the human warmth to meet you where you are.

## Extended Bio (about / bio section)

Christine trained and practiced at some of Boston's leading institutions — Boston Children's Hospital, Harvard Medical School, McLean Hospital, and the Boston Child Study Center — before building a practice focused on what she loves most: working directly with parents.

She is a Certified PCIT (Parent-Child Interaction Therapy) Therapist and is trained in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT). Her research background — rooted in social cognition, emotion regulation, and developmental neuroscience — means her guidance is always evidence-based, never generic.

Multilingual and multicultural herself, Christine has lived and worked in Spain, Ecuador, and across the United States. She understands firsthand that raising children across cultures and languages adds a layer of beauty — and a layer of questions — that most parenting resources simply don't address.
